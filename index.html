Navagrahas & Nakṣatras

Navagrahas — the nine

Sūrya, Candra, Maṅgala, Budha, Guru, Śukra, Śani, Rāhu, Ketu. Seven are lights you can often see; Rāhu and Ketu are the lunar nodes — chāyā points (shadows), not stars. In this guide each graha shows its Raman rāśi, degree, nakṣatra, and pāda.

Nakṣatras — the 27 mansions

The ecliptic is divided into 27 nakṣatras (Aśvinī → Revatī), each 13°20′, with four pādas. Candra’s nakṣatra is the daily “star of the day” language of jyotiṣa; grahas sit in these mansions too. Turn on the Nakṣatra layer to see the belt on the sky.

Rāśis — the 12 signs

Twelve equal rāśis (Meṣa → Mīna) measure the same ecliptic in Raman sidereal. Zodiac belt layer draws them as a band; Planetarium mode keeps a red ecliptic spine for orientation.
